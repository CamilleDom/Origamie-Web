ABOUT ORIGAMI

HISTORY

Origami first appeared in 6th century Japan, and was originally practised by Buddhist monks.

This art was exclusively reserved for religion (representation of the gods) and ceremonies (death or marriage). It is now practised for pleasure!


ON PAPER

DID YOU KNOW?

A sheet of paper can only be folded seven times on itself...

And if you could fold it forty-two times on itself, that would be equivalent to the distance from the Earth to the Sun!

Paper first appeared in China and then Japan, but it was Islam that really spread this medium (initially to spread the Koran).


FAMILIES OF PAPER

  • Printing, writing and communication (paper for newspapers and magazines)
  • Office (xerography, laser, inkjet)
  • Writing
  • Packaging (corrugated or flexible kraft, coated on one side, etc.)
  • Sanitary and domestic use (toilet paper, handkerchiefs, medical paper for sterilisation bags, wipes).
  • Industrial and special papers (cigarette, decorative, tracing paper, crystal, greaseproof, coin, etc.).
  • But also cardboard of all kinds or labels!
